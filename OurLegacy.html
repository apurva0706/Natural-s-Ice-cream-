Our Legacy — Naturals Ice Cream

Taste the original.

The story of how a fruit vendor's son built Naturals Ice Cream — one real, whole fruit at a time.

Raghunandan Kamath grew up near Mangaluru, helping his father sell mangoes. He learned to tell a ripe fruit from a stubborn one just by feel, long before he had any idea it would shape his life.

Years later, working in his brother's eatery in Bombay, he kept asking himself one question: ice cream already came in fruit flavours, so why did almost none of it use real fruit? That question became Naturals.

"If I want to sell ice cream, I should invest in trees." Late Shri Raghunandan Kamath, founder, Naturals Ice Cream

How it started

In 1984, Kamath opened a small shop in Juhu Scheme, Mumbai. Unsure if people would come just for ice cream, he sold pav bhaji as the main dish and ice cream on the side — twelve flavours to start. Within a year, the ice cream didn't need the pav bhaji anymore. People were lining up for it on its own.

Still the same today

That's it. Three ingredients, the same ones since day one. No shortcuts, no artificial flavours, no added colour — just fruit, the way Kamath always insisted it should be.

What hasn't changed
  • MilkFrom the same vendor for over 40 years.
  • FruitReal, fresh, sourced from each fruit's best-known region.
  • SugarPharma-grade, pure enough to use in medicine.
